# What is Weara?

Weara is a two-sided AI styling marketplace for African women.

One surface. One session.
She asks "what should I wear?" and the answer pulls from:
- Her own wardrobe
- Kantamanto thrift sellers near her
- Emerging and luxury African fashion brands

---

## The Problem

### For the buyer

She saves outfits on Pinterest. But the inspiration never translates.
Different body. Different wardrobe. Different context.
The gap between the mood board and her actual closet is never bridged.

She scrolls Instagram and Facebook stores looking for specific pieces.
The algorithm surfaces whoever has the most followers.
The boutique with 200 followers and the perfect dress never appears.
She scrolls for 45 minutes and buys nothing or settles.

When she shops Kantamanto she goes looking for something specific
and discovery is physical — digging through bales with no search,
no filter, no way to know if the piece exists two stalls away.

She bookmarks DMs. Screenshots Instagram stories.
Twenty-four hours later the post is gone and so is the seller's contact.

### For the seller

Instagram and Facebook's algorithm rewards reach, not quality.
A small Kantamanto seller or a new IG boutique with genuine taste
gets buried under accounts that already have mass followings.

The only storefront they have is a story that disappears in 24 hours.
No search. No persistence. No way for the right customer to find them
unless she already follows them.

Unsold stock sits. Then it gets bagged, exported, or burned.

---

## The Insight

She does not shop in categories.
She is one person dressing for one life.

Pinterest gives her the dream. Instagram buries the sellers.
Her wardrobe holds the pieces but not the logic.
Weara closes all three gaps in one session.

---

## Two Sides

### Buyers (end-users) — free
Black and African women, 22–40.
Accra. Lagos. Nairobi. London. New York.

What she gets:
- AI styling brain trained on her wardrobe, occasions, body, and undertone
- Virtual style avatar — see outfits on a figure that looks like her
  before she leaves the house
- Pinterest-to-closet bridge — her saved inspiration matched to
  pieces she actually owns or can actually buy
- Outfit suggestions that pull from her closet AND real sellers in one feed
- Small sellers she would never find by scrolling alone
- Group closet for aso-ebi coordination with family

### Sellers — pay a success fee only
Two types:

**Kantamanto thrift sellers**
Time-poor. Photo-skill-poor. Buried by the algorithm.
What they get:
- Persistent searchable storefront that never disappears
- Weara photographs and lists their inventory (managed-listing)
- Mobile-money-native payouts (MoMo, Telecel Cash)
- Customers who arrive already styled and self-qualified
- Visibility that does not depend on follower count
- Less unsold stock rotting or burning

**Emerging + luxury African fashion brands**
Great inventory. Real aesthetic. No algorithmic reach.
What they get:
- Their pieces shown inside styled outfit recommendations
- A curated audience that already loves their aesthetic
- Discovery that does not depend on how many followers they have
- No Meta ads. No monthly fees. Pay only when something sells.
- Diaspora customer reach

The payer is the seller. The buyer is always free.
