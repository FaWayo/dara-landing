# Weara Website Design Direction

---

## Goal

Create an editorial fashion landing page that converts visitors into waitlist signups.
Two audiences. One page. Each must feel seen.

Buyer: "This was made for me."
Seller: "This will grow my business."

---

## Overall Feeling

Fashion magazine meets modern interactive web.
Think: a Lagos creative studio and a London fashion flat had a website.
Dark, confident, editorial. Not warm-earth-tones African cliché.

---

## Color Palette

| Name        | Hex       | Role                                      |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------------------------------|
| Space Cadet | #2B2D42   | Primary background. Dark, authoritative.  |
| Cadet Grey  | #8D99AE   | Secondary text. Muted elegance.           |
| Ghost White | #EDF2F4   | Primary text on dark. Clean contrast.     |
| Imperial Red| #EF233C   | Primary CTA. Urgency. Energy.             |
| Crimson     | #D90429   | CTA hover state. Deeper action.           |

Usage rules:
- Space Cadet (#2B2D42) is the dominant background color
- Ghost White (#EDF2F4) is the primary text color on dark backgrounds
- Imperial Red (#EF233C) is used ONLY for CTAs, key highlights, and moments
  that demand action — never decorative
- Crimson (#D90429) is the hover/active state for red elements only
- Cadet Grey (#8D99AE) for secondary text, labels, metadata
- No gradient blobs. No glassmorphism overuse.
- Red is earned. Use it once per section maximum.

## Typography

- Editorial. Elegant. Confident.
- Large headings — think magazine cover scale
- Primary font: a high-contrast serif for headlines
  (suggestion: Playfair Display or Editorial New)
- Secondary font: clean geometric sans for body
  (suggestion: Inter or DM Sans)
- Ghost White on Space Cadet backgrounds
- Never use red for body text

---

## Layout

- Magazine-inspired. Not card-heavy.
- Full-bleed photography sections
- Asymmetric editorial grids where it earns it
- Mobile-first. Every section designed on 390px first.

---

## Motion Rules

- Movement should reveal, not distract
- Entrance animations: fade-up on scroll, clean and fast (0.4–0.6s)
- Hero: one strong animated moment (collage, image reveal, or text stagger)
- No infinite looping animations
- No excessive parallax
- No movement for movement's sake
- Framer Motion only — no CSS animation soup

---

## Photography Direction

- Photography first. No illustration-heavy design.
- Real women. Real Accra/Lagos/diaspora settings.
- Thrift and luxury shown together — never separate worlds
- Natural light preferred
- Skin tones, hair, and cultural occasions are not edge cases

---

## Avoid

- Corporate SaaS language or layout
- Too much whitespace (this is editorial, not minimal)
- Gradient blobs
- Overusing glassmorphism
- Generic stock photography of women shopping
- Anything that could be mistaken for a Western resale app
